Tokyo

Japan’s capital and largest city


Nestled in the heart of Japan, Tokyo emerges as a captivating tapestry of past and present, where the ancient whispers of Edo mingle harmoniously with the vibrant rhythms of a modern metropolis. Once a humble village, Tokyo has evolved into a pulsating center of innovation, seamlessly blending traditional Japanese customs with cutting-edge technology. Its skyline, adorned with architectural marvels, serves as a testament to its boundless spirit and relentless pursuit of progress. Within this bustling urban landscape, visitors are beckoned to explore a kaleidoscope of experiences, from the tranquil sanctuaries of historic temples to the neon-lit streets of bustling districts. With iconic landmarks such as the majestic Tokyo Tower, the revered Senso-ji Temple, and the exclusive boutiques of Ginza, Tokyo promises an unforgettable journey where every corner reveals a new facet of its captivating character.

Top things to do in Tokyo


Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden


Shinjuku Gyoen, built upon the former grounds of Lord Naito's private residence during the Edo era, stands as a testament to Japan's historical evolution. Completed in 1906 as an imperial garden, it transitioned into a national treasure post-World War II, boasting an expansive 58.3 hectares (144 acres) and a 3.5-kilometer circumference. With its harmonious blend of Formal Garden, Landscape Garden, and Japanese Traditional Garden styles, Shinjuku Gyoen holds a revered status as one of the most significant green spaces from the Meiji era. During springtime, the garden's splendor reaches its peak, attracting visitors from far and wide to witness its mesmerizing cherry blossoms in full bloom.

Senso-ji Temple


Senso-ji Temple, nestled in Tokyo's Asakusa district, holds a profound legacy rooted in the legend of two brothers and their divine encounter with the goddess of mercy, Kannon. This ancient temple, adorned with intricate architectural details and crowned by a majestic pagoda, stands as a revered symbol of Japan's spiritual heritage. Welcoming millions of visitors annually, Senso-ji offers a sacred haven where pilgrims and tourists alike seek blessings, immerse themselves in Buddhist traditions, and traverse the bustling Nakamise-dori, a vibrant street leading to the temple's gates. A visit to Senso-ji Temple is an invitation to journey through Japan's rich cultural tapestry and connect with the timeless essence of its spiritual legacy.

1-2 hours

Meiji Jingu Shrine


Meiji Jingu Shrine stands as a timeless testament to Japan's rich cultural heritage and reverence for its imperial legacy. Enshrining the spirits of Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken, this sacred sanctuary beckons visitors with its majestic presence framed by two imposing gates, symbolizing a gateway to the divine. Constructed in 1920 and meticulously restored after sustaining damage during the tumult of World War II, Meiji Jingu Shrine exudes an aura of tranquility and spiritual resonance. Stepping through its hallowed precincts, visitors are enveloped in a serene atmosphere, where the whispers of ancient rituals and the rustle of sacred trees intertwine, offering solace and reflection amidst the vibrant pulse of modern Tokyo.


Duration:

1-2 hours
